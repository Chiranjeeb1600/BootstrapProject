How to Join?

You can join this great human movement towards a sustainable human future, by writing about your own effort to help in the Cause. The effort can be as simple as cleaning your neighborhood or may be as big as organizing members of your society to plant multiple trees or develop a whole new forest. A Simple or big project does not matter, as long as you keep your faith in the movement, and keep on providing your best effort to build this world a better place to live.

We are the first generation to feel the effect of climate change and the last generation who can do something about it.

Barak Obama, Former US President

Few Testimonies

Duc Mien, Ho Chi Minh City, Vietnam

I live in a city neighborhood where though there is a ban on plastic, but there is lot of use of Plastics. Also, the people don’t dispose off the plastic bags responsibly, so they litter around, and get choked in the Animal gut. So, this last weekend, our family decided to clean our neighborhood, we went around cleaning our building, nearby roads, gathered plastics, and various other waste, and segregated them and disposed them off in a proper manner, seeing our effort, some of our neighbors had also joined. We plan to continue this effort every fortnight.

Sri Ram Sundar, Chennai, India

I live in a city where the constant construction has led to very little space for plants to grow. Over the year my city area has also seen less and less rains, which has led to the drop in Ground water level. In our society we have identified a small area where we along with our families go every week to plant some trees or take care of those planted trees. Every family has planted at least two trees in this area and each of us take turn every morning/evening of take care of these plants. We take help from local horticulturist to help us know how to keep these plants healthy. We hope in the coming years we won’t be facing the water shortage and will have fresh air to breath in.

Gathii Mwangi, Nairobi, Kenya

I live in a small town where there is a range of chemical factories that dispose-off their waste in the nearby river. The river is a lifeline for the whole town, as its water is used by local villagers who live along the river-side to take bath, wash animals and wash their cloths. The river also is fed in a small dam downstream, where its water is processed for drinking water and supply to the local population. Over the years the processing plant is producing less a less water and the water quality has also deteriorated. We have tried to request the factories to process the waste before they put it in the river or put the waste in some other place, also we had requested the local villagers along the river side to not pollute the water. But both of these pleas have fallen to deaf ears, as they don’t have any alternate to this. I organized a small society called ‘Protect-River’ and now we have over 500 members in it, some of them are from the near-by towns also who also use this river water downstream for there home use, we have already put petition to the local government to move these factories to an alternate location and provide some water connections to these local villagers to help them do the cores at there own residence. We have got some encouraging response from local government, and we hope to continue this movement forward to reach a conclusive solution.
